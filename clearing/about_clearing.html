Clearing — iOS Dashboard

What it does

Four things.
Nothing more.

Most apps compete for your attention the moment you open them. Clearing was built around a different idea — give you exactly what you came for, then get out of the way.

01
Time & greeting

The time and date, with a greeting that changes through the day. Morning, afternoon, evening, night — each with its own closing line.

02
Live weather

Current conditions written in plain language. Temperature, wind, sky. No charts, no hourly forecasts, no noise.

03
Dressed for conditions

Based on how the weather actually feels, not just the temperature. What to wear, whether to bring an umbrella, when the UV is high. Written plainly.

04
App launch

A quiet line at the bottom. Type or speak any app name and launch it in one tap — without leaving the calm of Clearing or returning to the cluttered home screen.

The experience

A screen that
does less.

Clearing adapts to your environment. The theme shifts automatically with the sunrise and sunset, moving between warm dark and warm light as the day progresses. You can override it at any time with a single tap.

The weather paragraph is written in prose — not icons, not numbers, not data tables. It reads like something a person would say to you in the morning.

A Story

A morning,
uninterrupted.

You pick up your phone. You want to know two things before you leave the house:
weather, and whether to bring a jacket. Here is what happens next.

"It is 8:14 AM on Tuesday, April 22 in New York. The weather is partly cloudy with a temperature of 12°C (54°F). A gentle breeze is blowing at 16 kph (10 mph, 8 knots). A light jacket or sweater would serve you well today. Have a beautiful day."

  • — You have what you came for. Weather, temperature, wind, advice.
  • — You remember you need to check your calendar. You tap the search line, type "cal", tap Calendar. Done.
  • — You put your phone down. You did not open Instagram. You did not read three news headlines. You are already out the door.

An app that thinks different

A gap no one
has filled.

Minimal weather apps give you context but no utility. Minimal launchers give you utility but no context. Full-featured apps give you everything — and take your attention with it. Clearing occupies the only position that combines genuine contextual awareness with a deliberate commitment to focus.
